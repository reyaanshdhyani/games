🧠 EINSTEIN'S BIG DISCOVERY

In 1905, a young scientist named Albert Einstein discovered something nobody expected: the faster you move, the slower time passes for you.

This is called Time Dilation — and it's completely real! We've measured it. Here's the wild part: if you could fly near the speed of light, 1 year for you = hundreds of years on Earth.

👨‍🚀 REAL PROOF: ASTRONAUTS DO THIS!
🛸

GPS satellites orbit Earth at high speed. Because of time dilation, their clocks run slightly slower. Engineers must correct for this EVERY DAY — otherwise your maps would be off by miles!

👨‍🚀

ISS astronauts travel at 28,000 km/h. After 6 months in space, they return 0.007 seconds younger than people who stayed on Earth. Tiny — but real time travel!

🪐 GRAVITY ALSO BENDS TIME!

Speed isn't the only thing that slows time. Gravity does too! Einstein discovered that massive objects like planets and stars bend space — and time itself!

The stronger the gravity, the slower time passes. So time actually passes a tiny bit slower on the ground than high up in the mountains — because you're closer to Earth's heavy mass!

At the edge (event horizon) of a black hole, time stops COMPLETELY from the perspective of a distant observer. 1 second there = billions of years far away. The ultimate time machine!

If you fell into a black hole, you'd look frozen to someone watching from far away — they'd never actually see you cross the edge due to extreme time dilation!

⚫ THE ULTIMATE TIME MACHINE: BLACK HOLES

A black hole is a place in space where gravity is so incredibly strong that nothing can escape — not even light. And here's the wildest part: near a black hole, time nearly stops.

If you hovered just outside a black hole for 1 hour, thousands of years could pass on Earth. This is science fact, not science fiction!

The biggest black holes are called supermassive. The one at the center of our galaxy (Milky Way) is 4 million times heavier than the Sun!

We photographed one!

In 2019, scientists took the first ever photo of a black hole. It's 6.5 billion times heavier than the Sun and 55 million light-years away!

🍝
Spaghettification

If you fell INTO a black hole, you'd get stretched like spaghetti! The gravity pulls your feet much harder than your head. Scientists actually call this "spaghettification."

🌈
Light bends around them

Black holes are so heavy they actually bend the path of light around them. You can see things that are behind a black hole because light curves around it!
